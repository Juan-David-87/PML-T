CRISP-ML Phase

Business Understanding

Definition of the business problem, project objectives, research questions, feasibility analysis, and machine learning approach.

Research Questions

Key Questions Guiding the Project

The project is structured around a set of analytical questions designed to better understand the relationship between connectivity conditions, territorial inequalities, and the risk of digital isolation in Colombia.

Which socioeconomic variables are most strongly correlated with low levels of digital connectivity across Colombian municipalities?

Is it possible to predict the level of digital isolation risk (High, Medium, or Low) based on current infrastructure, territorial conditions, and demographic indicators?

Key Questions

Why Were These Questions Defined?

Socioeconomic Correlation

One of the main goals is to determine whether socioeconomic indicators such as population density, education levels, income conditions, or geographic location are directly associated with low connectivity levels.

Predictive Risk Analysis

Another key question is whether it is possible to predict the level of digital isolation risk (High, Medium, or Low) based on current connectivity infrastructure and demographic conditions.

Machine Learning Approach

Supervised Classification Problem

From a machine learning perspective, the project is defined as a supervised learning classification problem. The target variable corresponds to the level of digital isolation risk, categorized into High, Medium, or Low levels.

The system will analyze patterns within connectivity, infrastructure, demographic, and territorial data in order to train models capable of classifying municipalities according to their vulnerability level.

Feasibility Analysis

Availability of Open Data

Colombia provides robust open datasets through institutions such as MinTIC and national statistical entities. These datasets include connectivity indicators, infrastructure coverage, population distribution, and territorial information.

However, one of the main challenges of the project is integrating heterogeneous data sources, especially when combining connectivity indicators with geographic, demographic, and socioeconomic data for machine learning analysis.

Project Objectives

Main Goals of the Project

The primary objective of this project is to identify Colombian municipalities with a high probability of digital isolation through the use of machine learning techniques and open connectivity datasets.

The project also aims to support strategic decision-making processes related to telecommunications infrastructure, digital inclusion policies, and regional technological development.

Connectivity Analysis

Analyze connectivity indicators and regional inequalities.

Predictive Modeling

Develop classification models capable of identifying risk levels.

Infrastructure Support

Support public and private investment planning processes.

Risks and Limitations

Challenges of the Project

Data Availability

Some municipalities may contain incomplete, outdated, or inconsistent connectivity records, affecting the accuracy of the analysis.

Geographic Bias

Rural and remote regions may present limited digital reporting infrastructure, generating representation bias.

Dynamic Infrastructure Changes

Connectivity infrastructure evolves constantly, which may reduce the long-term validity of the predictive models.

Model Generalization

Machine learning models may not fully capture all social, political, or territorial factors influencing digital isolation.

Expected Impact

Social and Technological Contribution

The project seeks to contribute to the reduction of digital inequality in Colombia by providing analytical tools capable of identifying vulnerable regions.

The results may support governmental institutions, telecommunications companies, and social organizations in the planning of digital infrastructure investments and inclusion strategies.

Real-World Justification

Why Is This Problem Important?

Digital connectivity has become a critical factor for education, economic development, communication, healthcare access, and social inclusion.

In Colombia, many rural and geographically isolated regions still experience limited internet access and weak technological infrastructure. This situation directly affects equal access to opportunities and public services.

By applying machine learning methodologies, this project aims to provide a scalable analytical solution capable of identifying territorial patterns associated with digital isolation risk.
