CRISP-ML Methodology

This section describes the CRISP-ML methodology used throughout the lifecycle of the BrechaDigital CO project. The methodology provides a structured and iterative framework for understanding the business problem, preparing the data, engineering machine learning models, evaluating performance, and deploying analytical solutions focused on identifying municipalities at risk of digital isolation in Colombia.

Explore Project Phases

What is CRISP-ML?

CRISP-ML(Q) is an evolution of the traditional CRISP-DM methodology, specifically adapted for modern Machine Learning projects. Unlike traditional data mining methodologies, CRISP-ML emphasizes model quality assurance, reproducibility, deployment, monitoring, and continuous improvement.

In this project, the methodology was applied to analyze territorial, institutional, and digital government indicators in Colombia in order to classify municipalities according to their level of digital isolation risk.

CRISP-ML Project Phases

The project follows a structured lifecycle composed of multiple iterative and interconnected phases.

Business & Data Understanding

The first phase focused on understanding the digital divide problem in Colombia and translating business needs into analytical objectives.

  • Identification of the digital isolation problem
  • Definition of project objectives
  • Research question formulation
  • Dataset exploration and feasibility analysis
  • Understanding territorial and institutional variables

Data Engineering

During this phase, the dataset was cleaned, transformed, normalized, and prepared for machine learning implementation.

  • Data cleaning and standardization
  • Missing value handling
  • Feature engineering
  • Encoding categorical variables
  • Scaling and normalization

Model Engineering

Multiple machine learning models were explored to identify, classify, and analyze municipalities with digital isolation risk.

  • K-Means clustering for territorial classification
  • DBSCAN for anomaly and density detection
  • PCA for dimensionality reduction
  • Model training and parameter adjustment
  • Performance validation

Evaluation, Deployment & Monitoring

The final stages focused on validating model quality, deploying the Flask application, and ensuring future scalability and monitoring.

  • Cross-validation and evaluation metrics
  • Risk and bias analysis
  • Flask web application deployment
  • Continuous monitoring and maintenance
  • Documentation and reproducibility

Machine Learning Models Used

The project incorporates three main unsupervised learning models to analyze digital isolation risk patterns across Colombian municipalities.

K-Means

Used to classify municipalities into different digital isolation risk groups such as High, Medium, and Low risk.

DBSCAN

Used to detect isolated municipalities, anomalies, and geographically vulnerable clusters.

PCA

Used to reduce dimensionality and simplify highly correlated digital government indicators before clustering analysis.

Project Goal

The main goal of BrechaDigital CO is to support evidence-based decision making by identifying municipalities with high vulnerability to digital exclusion.

Through the integration of machine learning, open government data, and territorial analysis, the platform helps visualize technological inequalities and contributes to future digital inclusion strategies in Colombia.
