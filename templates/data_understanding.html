Data Understanding

Comprehensive exploratory analysis of the Digital Government Index — Historical dataset following the CRISP-ML(Q) methodology. Each activity is documented with its objective, rationale, and key findings.

Activity 1

Dataset Exploration

Initial inspection of the dataset structure, dimensions, and content to understand its organization and analytical potential.

What

Basic dataset profiling: shape, data types, and sample records.

Why

Establish a solid foundational understanding before any deeper analysis or preprocessing.

Key Insights

Historical data across multiple years, territorial granularity (municipality/department), and institutional entities.

  • ✓ Dataset Shape: [rows, 18 columns]
  • ✓ Temporal Coverage: Multiple years (vigencia)
  • ✓ Granularity: Municipality, Department, and Institutional level
  • ✓ Duplicated records: Detected and documented

Variable Analysis

Detailed classification and inspection of numerical and categorical variables.

What

Classification and profiling of all variables.

Why

Understand the nature of each feature to define appropriate preprocessing and modeling strategies.

Activity 3

Missing Value Analysis

Systematic evaluation of data completeness and patterns of missingness.

What

Quantify and analyze missing values per variable.

Why

Missing data can bias models and must be understood before deciding treatment strategies.

  • ✓ Missingness patterns identified — some related to non-participating entities in specific years.
  • ✓ High missing columns flagged for potential removal or special treatment.
  • ✓ Missingness documented as possible domain knowledge (non-reported data).
Activity 4

Data Quality Evaluation

Assessment of consistency, accuracy, and reliability of the dataset.

  • ✓ Duplicate detection & naming standardization
  • ✓ Territorial naming consistency reviewed
  • ✓ Domain constraint validation (scores between 0-100, quintiles 1-5)
  • ✓ Overall quality: Acceptable after light cleaning
Activity 5

Statistical Summary & Correlation Analysis

Descriptive statistics and relationship analysis between variables.

Descriptive Statistics

Mean, std, min, max, quartiles, and distribution shape of key performance indicators.

Correlation Analysis

Preliminary correlation matrix to identify relationships between performance metrics and group benchmarks.
