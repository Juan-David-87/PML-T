Data Understanding

This section presents the exploratory analysis performed on the selected dataset used in the BrechaDigital CO project. The purpose of this phase is to understand the structure, quality, completeness, and analytical potential of the information before beginning preprocessing and machine learning activities. The analysis includes exploratory data analysis, variable inspection, missing value evaluation, data quality assessment, statistical summaries, and correlation analysis.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

An exploratory data analysis (EDA) was conducted to understand the structure, organization, and analytical characteristics of the dataset before future preprocessing and predictive modeling stages.

The exploratory process focused on understanding dataset dimensions, identifying variable types, examining historical observations, and evaluating territorial information associated with municipalities, departments, and institutional entities across Colombia.

Additional inspection activities included reviewing duplicated observations, detecting possible inconsistencies, and understanding the distribution of digital government indicators across different territorial entities.

Initial findings suggested that the dataset presents a structured tabular organization suitable for exploratory analysis and future machine learning preparation. Historical observations also support comparisons between municipalities and departments over time.

Variable Analysis

A variable analysis was conducted to understand the meaning, interpretation, and analytical role of the features contained in the dataset. The selected information includes both numerical and categorical variables associated with territorial, historical, and institutional characteristics.

Categorical variables provide contextual information related to municipalities, departments, entities, and administrative classifications, while numerical variables represent digital government performance indicators and historical measurements.

Understanding the role of each variable helps support future preprocessing, feature selection, and machine learning preparation.

Numerical Variables

Numerical variables represent scores, measurements, identifiers, and historical indicators.

  • ENTITY_SCORE
  • VALIDITY
  • MUNICIPALITY_ID
  • DEPARTMENT_ID
  • EVEN_GROUP_AVERAGE
  • EVEN_GROUP_MAXIMUM
  • EVEN_GROUP_PERCENTILE
Categorical Variables

Categorical variables describe territorial and institutional classifications.

  • MUNICIPALITY
  • DEPARTMENT
  • ENTITY
  • ORDER
  • INDEX
  • LEGAL_NATURE
  • SECTOR

Missing Value Analysis

A missing value analysis was conducted to evaluate the completeness and reliability of the dataset. The inspection process aimed to identify incomplete observations and determine whether missing information could negatively affect future analytical processes.

Some historical and institutional records contained incomplete values, especially in territorial or administrative fields. These missing observations may reflect unavailable reporting, historical inconsistencies, or data collection limitations.

At this stage, missing values were documented and reviewed in order to preserve transparency and support future preprocessing decisions such as imputation, replacement, or removal strategies.

Understanding missing information is important to improve data consistency and minimize reliability problems during future predictive modeling stages.

Data Quality Evaluation

Data quality evaluation focused on assessing the reliability, consistency, and usability of the selected dataset.

The evaluation process reviewed duplicated records, formatting inconsistencies, historical coherence, incomplete observations, and territorial naming conventions.

Particular attention was given to ensuring that municipalities, departments, and institutional information remained consistent across observations in order to improve interpretability and future analytical reliability.

Overall, the dataset presented acceptable quality standards, although minor inconsistencies were identified and documented for future preprocessing stages.

Statistical Summary

Statistical summaries were considered to better understand the behavior of numerical variables and institutional performance indicators included in the dataset.

Descriptive measures such as averages, minimum values, maximum values, percentile distributions, and variability were reviewed to better understand territorial digital development patterns.

Statistical exploration helps identify performance differences between municipalities and departments and supports future preprocessing and predictive analysis.

These summaries provide a clearer understanding of how digital government indicators behave across different territorial contexts.

Correlation Analysis

Correlation analysis was preliminarily considered to explore possible relationships between numerical variables associated with institutional performance and digital government indicators.

The objective of this analysis is to identify whether certain variables present similar behavioral patterns that may contribute to future predictive tasks and feature selection.

Preliminary exploration suggests that some digital performance indicators may present related tendencies due to the measurement methodology and evaluation criteria used within the dataset.

Correlation analysis represents an important step toward future machine learning implementation because it helps understand relationships between variables and supports better model preparation decisions.
