Problem Statement

Understanding Digital Isolation Risk in Colombia

The main objective of this project is to identify and classify municipalities or regions in Colombia with a high probability of digital isolation in order to support decision-making processes related to telecommunications infrastructure investment.

Despite the growth of digital technologies, many regions still experience limited access to stable connectivity, technological infrastructure, and digital services. This creates territorial inequalities that directly affect education, communication, economic opportunities, and access to information.

Research Questions

Key Questions Guiding the Project

The project is structured around a set of analytical questions designed to better understand the relationship between connectivity conditions, territorial inequalities, and the risk of digital isolation in Colombia.

Which socioeconomic variables are most strongly correlated with low levels of digital connectivity across Colombian municipalities?

Is it possible to predict the level of digital isolation risk (High, Medium, or Low) based on current infrastructure, territorial conditions, and demographic indicators?

Key Questions

Why Were These Questions Defined?

Socioeconomic Correlation

One of the main goals is to determine whether socioeconomic indicators such as population density, education levels, income conditions, or geographic location are directly associated with low connectivity levels.

Predictive Risk Analysis

Another key question is whether it is possible to predict the level of digital isolation risk (High, Medium, or Low) based on current connectivity infrastructure and demographic conditions.

Machine Learning Approach

Supervised Classification Problem

From a machine learning perspective, the project is defined as a supervised learning classification problem. The target variable corresponds to the level of digital isolation risk, categorized into High, Medium, or Low levels.

The system will analyze patterns within connectivity, infrastructure, demographic, and territorial data in order to train models capable of classifying municipalities according to their vulnerability level.

Feasibility Analysis

Availability of Open Data

Colombia provides robust open datasets through institutions such as MinTIC and national statistical entities. These datasets include connectivity indicators, infrastructure coverage, population distribution, and territorial information.

However, one of the main challenges of the project is integrating heterogeneous data sources, especially when combining connectivity indicators with geographic, demographic, and socioeconomic data for machine learning analysis.
